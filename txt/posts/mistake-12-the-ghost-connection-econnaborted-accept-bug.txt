  SPECIAL -- IPC MISTAKE: THE GHOST CONNECTION ECONNABORTED ACCEPT BUG
  Mistake 12: The Ghost Connection ECONNABORTED accept bug
  ============================================================================================


      The OS completes the TCP handshake *before* your code calls `accept()`. If a client connects and then instantly sends a kill signal (Ctrl+C) before `accept()` runs, the connection dies in the queue. When `accept()` finally grabs it, it returns `-1`. Without error checking, the server blindly calls `write(-1)`, failing silently or crashing. On Linux, a blocking `accept()` might freeze forever waiting for a new connection. The fix is making the socket non-blocking (`O_NONBLOCK`).
